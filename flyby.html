Flyby Spacecrafts

What are they?

Spacecrafts that follow a trajectory that is not a closed loop around a planet.

Purpose:

Designed to fly by a planet or other celestial object of scientific interest, collecting data with their array of sensors and instruments. Some flyby trajectories were cleverly designed to bring the spacecraft in close proximity to a series of planets.

Examples:

The four most succesful flyby space crafts are (in order of launch):

  1. Pioneer 10
  2. Pioneer 11
  3. Voyager 1
  4. Voyager 2
